### Research Experience
     
#### Michigan Technological University (June 2022 - Present)

Currently, I am working as a <strong>Research Assistant</strong> for Dr. Charles Wallace of the Michigan Technological University Computer Science Department. Primarily, my work entails declarative logic software development with Alloy and Spin for OECD <a style="color: #f56a6a; !important" href="https://www.oecd-ilibrary.org/docserver/3afe6ba5-en.pdf?expires=1659488696&id=id&accname=guest&checksum=95BEF3962A3B97191CEE4E2099CE3F68"><i>Rules as Code</i></a>-based project modeling Michigan statutory expungement law, <a style="color: #f56a6a; !important" href="http://www.legislature.mi.gov/(S(jnawlz0cg1faphkrzgif5sua))/mileg.aspx?page=getobject&objectname=mcl-780-621">MCL 780.621</a>, which allows old convictions to be removed from individual's criminal records. My work on the project also includes comprehensive research on the text and implications of the statute, utilizing linear temporal logic to write mathematical models of the statute, and conducting interviews with attorneys, judges, and paralegals on different interpretations of the statue.
      
#### Oberlin College and Conservatory (October 2021 - June 2022)

In 2021-2022 I worked as a <strong>Research Assistant</strong> on the Oberlin College History Design Lab's <a style="color: #f56a6a; !important" href="https://afamwomen.historydesignlab.org/"><i>African American Women Intellectuals</i></a> project. As a member of the project team, I utilized the college archives to write scholarly biographies of female African American Oberlin graduates under the direction of Dr. Renee Romano.
 
      
#### Bowling Green State University (May 2021 - September 2021)

In the summer of 2021, I worked as a <strong>Research Assistant</strong> for Dr. Melissa Miller of the Bowling Green State University Political Science Department. As a member of the <i>Trailblazing Women in Ohio Politics</i> research team, I utilized archival newspaper records to compile a complete career history for women who have broken electoral barriers in Ohio. My research formed the basis of biographical narratives about US Representative Marcy Kaptur and former Ohio Attorney General and State Auditor Betty Montgomery that will appear on the project's website in March 2023. My research was also utilized by Dr. Miller prior to interviewing each trailblazer for a public television documentary.
