Service

Oberlin College and Conservatory (October 2021 - Present)

In the winter of 2022, I am working as a Research Assistant on the Oberlin College History Department's African American Women Intellectuals project. As a member of this project, I am utilizing the college archives to study scholarly biographies of female African American Oberlin graduates under direction of the history department chair, Dr. Annemarie Sammartino.

During the fall of 2021 and spring of 2022, I am working as a Data Structures Tutor for Oberlin data structures lectures (CSCI 151), conducting one-on-one and group sessions in which we cover topics including queues, stacks, heaps, trees, hash maps, linked lists, sorting algorithms, and dynamic programming.

I am also working as a Chinese Tutor for Oberlin's elementary Chinese (CHIN 101) and intermediate Chinese lectures (CHIN 201), during the fall of 2021. This includes teaching tones, fundamental grammatical structures, and text comprehension.

Bowling Green State University (May 2021 - September 2021)

In the summer of 2021, I worked as a Research Assistant for the Bowling Green State University Political Science Department on the Trailblazing Women in Ohio Politics project. As a member of the research team, I cataloged newspaper and media articles and wrote reports for public website on women who have broken electoral barriers in Ohio including US Representative Marcy Kaptur and Ohio Attorney General and State Auditor Betty Montgomery, all under the direction of Dr. Melissa Miller and Dr. Shannon Orr.
